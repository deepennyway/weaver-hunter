Skills

Skills are the bread and butter of Torment of the Grey Mage, if there’s a bread and butter. If you want to do something and there are stakes involved, you have to use a skill. This ensures that your character is doing things you want them to do, and it also ensures that you get to roll dice every once in a while.

  • You can have any number of skills.
  • Each skill has the following attributes:
    • Rating: An abstraction of your skill level. The higher your Rating, the bigger the die (d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, or d20). You can always choose to use a smaller die.
    • Knack: If the skill is in your strong suit, you can spend a Fortune to roll it twice and choose which roll to use.
  • Most skills will have a Rating of d6, at least to begin with.

When you use a skill, you (usually, probably) want a high number. A high number lets you avoid dire consequences through a kind of banded success:

  • 1: You get taken out of the scene, and you have to spend time regrouping. Reduce that skill’s Confidence by 1.
  • 2-3: You get hurt. Lose 1 health, and reduce that skill’s Confidence by 1.
  • 4-5: You compromise yourself. Lock that skill, and reduce its Confidence by 1.
  • 6 or higher: You suffer no consequences. Add 1 to that skill’s Confidence.

Note that in all of these cases, you do what you set out to do; the roll just tells you what happens to you as a result.

You can only do something if it maps to one of your skills. If you want to do something and it doesn’t map to a skill you have, you have to add it to your character as a new skill.

Confidence

The more you use a skill successfully, the more confident you become in its use. But too much confidence can lead to mistakes. While a skill’s Confidence is 5, replace the “6 or higher” result for that skill with the following:

  • 6 or higher: You overextend yourself. Lock that skill. Reset its Confidence to 0. Something bad happens.

Confidence does nothing else for you. It cannot be reduced below 0.

Pulling Punches

When you use a skill, your rating tells you the biggest size of die you can roll. You can always choose to roll a smaller one, though, if you notice that your Confidence is getting too high. Doing this costs you nothing, but it does carry increased risk to your health.
