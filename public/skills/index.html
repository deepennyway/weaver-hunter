Skills

In the basic rules, your suits each do one thing and one thing only, and when a suit is locked, that entire sphere of action is no longer available to you until it is unlocked.

With this advanced rule, each suit provides a grouping of actions that you can use, called skills. Instead of an entire suit being locked, your rolls can result in individual skills becoming locked. This advanced rule will make your characters more resilient and versatile.

With this rule in play, each character begins with one skill in each suit.

For example, here’s Emily’s character record with this rule in play:

Name: Emily ◯ ◯ ◯
Spades [ sword ]
Clubs [ fire spell ]
Diamonds [ inspect ]
Hearts [ dash ]

At the start of the game, this rule should feel no different from the basic rules. On Emily’s turn she chooses to explore, and reveals a 3 of Spades. Instead of choosing Spades to deal with the challenge, Emily chooses sword. Then she rolls a die and assesses the outcome as normal.

Learning New Skills

With this rule in play, you learn a new skill in a given suit when you reveal the Jack from that suit. No other players gain this new skill; only the player that finds the Jack receives its benefits.

Continuing the above example, on her turn, Emily reveals a Jack of Spades, and acquires the Bow skill under the Spades suit. The other players at the table remain unchanged by this event.

Yes, this means you might have a game where one player finds all the Jacks and another finds none. As an option, you can “gift” your Jack to another player. The key takeaway here is that only one player benefits from each Jack.

Locking skills

With this rule, when you get the Lock outcome on a roll, you don’t lock the entire suit; instead, you lock the specific skill you used that turn. Conversely, when you spend your turn unlocking a suit, you instead choose a single skill to unlock that turn.

So if Emily uses Spades (Sword) on her turn and rolls a Lock, only the Sword skill is locked. She can still use Bow as normal. She can even use her Bow skill when she spends her turn attempting to unlock her Sword skill.
