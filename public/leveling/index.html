Leveling Up

In the basic rules, the game is over when the players either escape the map or are buried in its collapse. With this advanced rule, you can play again with a new area, keeping the same characters and watching them grow.

If you’re playing with the Skills and Suit Rating advanced rules, this is a great way to develop your characters’ abilities over time across multiple levels. If you’re playing with a Weaver too, this advanced rule also helps you tell longer stories.

Starting a New Level

When the group leaves an area, gather up all the cards, shuffle them together, and then lay out a new map face-down as if you were starting a new game. You can play the same size map as before, or change the map size to suit your needs in terms of time or pacing.

Everyone keeps the same character they had before, unless someone wants to change their character. I recommend letting players renaming their skills between levels as they figure out who their characters are, but it’s not a requirement.

Any characters that were defeated or incapacitated at the end of the previous level are restored to full health and all their skills are unlocked. Everyone else unlocks one skill and recovers one health.

Any items that were found in the previous level are lost. Maybe you had to leave them behind. Maybe you simply realize that they won’t be of use in this new environment.

Improving Your Mask

The more areas you escape, the farther you progress toward your goal of leaving this prison, the more resilient your mask becomes.

Up until now, we haven’t dealt with giving players more masks, because three marks is plenty to give us a sense of danger without feeling too fragile. But here’s an optional rule for you.

You increase your number of Masks in one of the following scenarios:

  • Speedrun: Your group leaves the area without removing any face cards from the map, other than the Aces.
  • Threat Neutralized: Your group removes all four Aces from the map after both Jokers have been revealed.
  • MVP: You remove all four Aces from the map by yourself.
  • Glutton For Punishment: Your group leaves the area with you having found only numbered cards.
  • Rotten Luck: You find both Jokers by yourself, before any Aces have been found.

Each player can only benefit from each condition once. This means that the maximum number of masks a player can have is 8.
