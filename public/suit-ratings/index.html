Suit Ratings

In the basic rules, suits don’t come with ratings; you always roll a single die, and you stick with that for the entire game. With this advanced rule, every suit instead begins with a rating of 1, which should be recorded when you create your character sheet. I recommend writing this down next to the name of the suit so you can keep track of it more easily.

Here’s Emily’s character record with this rule in play:

Name: Emily ◯ ◯ ◯
Spades 1 ◯
Clubs 1 ◯
Diamonds 1 ◯
Hearts 1 ◯

When you use a skill, you roll a number of dice equal to the skill’s suit rating. If you roll more than one die, you keep the highest roll from all your dice. If you roll more than one die, you keep the highest result; so if you roll a 4 and a 6, you keep the 6 and ignore the 4.

At the start of the game, this rule should feel no different from the basic rules. On Emily’s turn she chooses to explore, and reveals a 6 of Hearts. Emily chooses Diamonds to deal with the challenge, and then rolls a die and assesses the outcome as normal.

Suit Ratings and Skills: If you’re also using the Skills advanced rule, the rating for a suit applies to every skill in that suit. So if Emily has a rating of 2 in Diamonds, she rolls two dice when she uses any Diamonds skill.

Improving a Suit

With this rule in play, your rating in a given suit increases by 1 when all of the face cards in that suit are revealed. This improvement happens immediately, and for all players.

For example, on her turn, Emily reveals the Ace of Spades. The Jack, Queen, and King of spades are already revealed; because of this, Emily’s rating in Spades permanently increases to 2, allowing her to roll 2 dice instead of 1 whenever she uses the Spades suit.

When you roll multiple dice for a suit, you always keep only the highest die you rolled. So continuing the above example, if Emily uses Spades and she rolls a 4 and a 6, she uses the 6 to determine the outcome of her action that turn and ignores the 4.

Dangerous Challenges - No Great Risk

If you’re playing with this advanced rule, your character can ignore the challenge for any card whose number is equal to or less than your rating in that card’s suit.

For example, Emily’s Spades suit has a rating of 2. This means that if she draws a 2 of Spades on her turn, she doesn’t have to roll; her suit is strong enough to carry her through the area safely.
